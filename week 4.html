Child Labor Awareness – WEEK 4 (15-08-25 to 28-08-25)

Objective of the Activity Done

Awareness on child labor in rural areas.

Detailed Report

DAY 1

Visited rural areas to educate children and raise awareness on child labor.

DAY 2

  • Discussed the benefits of child education.
  • Education helps develop critical skills: decision making, mental agility, problem-solving, and logical thinking.
  • Poverty reduction, sense of accomplishment, more productivity, and greater discipline.

DAY 3

Continued discussions on the importance of child education in society.

DAY 4

Raised questions about child labor. Agriculture in India is the largest sector employing children to support family needs. Factors include unemployment, large families, poverty, and lack of parental education.

DAY 5

  • Explained the impacts of child labor.
  • Gathered information about children attending school versus working in fields.
  • Women shared their challenges and conditions.

DAY 6

Survey analysis revealed some children are not attending schools, and many are unaware of government schemes. Observed a general lack of awareness regarding child labor impacts.
