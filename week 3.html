Child Labor Awareness – WEEK 3 (08-07-25 to 09-08-25)

Objective of the Activity Done

In the third week we focused on creating awareness directly among children.

Detailed Report

DAY 1

Started the awareness program by collecting survey materials and preparing answers to common questions raised during previous weeks.

DAY 2

Started an awareness drive on child labour. Employment of children below a certain age limit is termed as child labour, which varies from country to country. In India, the age limit for children is 14 years, and any factory or industrial unit employing children below 14 years is illegal.

DAY 3

Most children were seen working in mechanic shops, so we started a survey to identify such children in nearby areas.

DAY 4

Spread awareness in shops by explaining why education is important for children.

DAY 5

  • Told slogans about child labour: "Child labour is like a disease that spreads", "Safe childhood is every child’s right", "Empower them instead of employing them", "Child labour is expanding day by day, and childhoods of children are getting worse".

DAY 6

Created charts on child labour and its importance around the school to educate children and improve their understanding of education.
